Improved Baselines with Representation Autoencoders

Jaskirat Singh1,2   Boyang Zheng3   Zongze Wu1   Richard Zhang1   Eli Shechtman1   Saining Xie3
1Adobe Research   2ANU   3New York University

TL;DR: We simplify and improve Representation Autoencoders. The result: over 10× faster convergence, with better reconstruction, better generation, and better performance on T2I and world models.

Better generation. Over 10× faster convergence than prior baselines (800 epochs). RAEv2 achieves state-of-the-art gFID and FDr6 in just 80 epochs, with no post-training.
Better reconstruction. A generalized formulation of RAE improves reconstruction without encoder finetuning or specialized data.
Efficient inference. When used with RAE, REPA itself can be reused for guidance — eliminating the need for a secondary weaker diffusion model (AutoGuidance) or an additional forward pass (CFG).

Key Findings

1

Pretrained encoders are more than their final layer. Aggregating the last $K$ encoder layers (simple addition) gives Pareto-optimal reconstruction and generation without any encoder finetuning or specialized training data.

2

RAE and REPA exhibit complementary mechanisms. RAE captures global semantics; REPA regularizes spatial structure. Using the same representation as both encoder and intermediate target consistently improves generation across 27 encoders — and lets stronger encoders (DINOv3-L) win on generation too.

3

REPA is x-prediction in RAE latent space. Re-parameterizing the DiT output as x-prediction lets the lightweight REPA head act as the weak baseline for internal guidance — no AutoGuidance model, no extra forward pass, halving NFEs at inference.

Overview

Representation Autoencoders (RAE) replace the traditional VAE in latent diffusion with a pretrained vision encoder (DINOv2, DINOv3, SigLIP, ...) plus a learned decoder. This is a step toward unified tokenization for both understanding and generation. But several practical issues persist: reconstruction lags behind specialized VAEs, RAE is incompatible with classifier-free guidance and relies on a separately-trained AutoGuidance model, and the encoder representation itself is underexplored — prior work always uses the final layer.

All three issues are addressed below by three independent design choices, each of which is training-free on the encoder side.

We investigate three design choices for representation autoencoders. Each is independently useful; together they produce RAEv2, a recipe with substantially faster convergence, better reconstruction, and simpler guidance.

  1. Generalized Representation Encoder: define the encoder output as the sum of the last $K$ pretrained-encoder layers, not just the final one.
  2. RAE + REPA are complementary: use the same representation as both encoder (RAE) and intermediate target (REPA).
  3. REPA as x-prediction: re-parameterize the DiT output so the REPA head itself provides guidance, removing the need for AutoGuidance or a second CFG forward pass.
